The US-Japan Energy Institute will focus on three main objectives

  1. Identify key topics for online training that focus on problem solving in the real world today
  2. Select key distinguished speakers from Japan and the United States that will offer short videos and papers (all translated into Japanese and English)
  3. Compile a history of US-Japan collaboration over a fifty year period — 1973-2023, highlighting important global and national events which have impacted national economies and national security
  4. Linkages within the website will allow easy access between the training modules, historical event and key speakers including a large library of publications in Japanese and English. While the website will be complex and comprehensive and serve as an archive for research purposes the training courses themselves will be easily accessible in modules which will take a trainee no more than one hour a week.

A full introductory course will include 11 modules — so the entire course can be taken in only 11 hours. Videos will be no longer than five minutes and articles can be read in less than 15 minutes. The aim is to take complex history and experience and make it discernible and practical to trainees (young and old) that need to have an accurate perspective of current US-Japan energy issues today.
